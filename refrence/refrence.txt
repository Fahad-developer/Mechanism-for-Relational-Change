Who We Are
Mechanism for Rational Change (MRC), established in 2015 under the Societies Registration Act, is a legally registered, women-led non-profit organization based in Khuzdar, Balochistan, Pakistan. Since its inception, MRC has been dedicated to empowering women, children, and youth through initiatives in education, leadership development, entrepreneurship, mental health awareness, climate action, and skill enhancement.
MRC’s unique working approach focuses on practicing ideas before scaling them. Through its model institution, the School of Scholars—the first girls’ school in Khuzdar introducing STEM and leadership education—MRC pilots programs at a community level before developing long-term projects. The organization also leads community-driven advocacy and capacity-building initiatives that promote gender equality, entrepreneurship, education access, and social justice.

Vision
A peaceful, educated, empowered and inclusive society where girls, youth and women lead change.
Mission
To empower girls, youth and women through education, leadership, advocacy, skills, technology and community-led development.

Girls Leadership Program ( feminist grant ) 
Duration: May 2022 – April 2024
About the Program
The Girls Leadership Program by the Mechanism for Rational Change (MRC) was a comprehensive initiative designed to empower young girls with leadership skills, confidence, and practical knowledge to become active contributors to their communities.
Guided by the vision of “Learning and Leading at Each Level,” the program focused on nurturing future female leaders in Balochistan by creating safe spaces for learning, leadership, advocacy, and community action.
Through this program, young girls were trained, mentored, and supported to understand social issues, raise their voices, and take practical steps for positive change in their schools and communities.
Objectives of the Program
The main objectives of the Girls Leadership Program were to:
•	Strengthen leadership and communication skills among young girls.
•	Promote critical thinking, teamwork, and decision-making.
•	Build confidence, self-awareness, and personal growth.
•	Encourage girls’ participation in education and community development.
•	Develop young girls as agents of positive social change.
•	Create a network of young female leaders in Khuzdar and Balochistan.
Program Structure
The program followed a structured and impactful approach:
1. Orientation and Selection
A transparent and merit-based selection process was carried out to identify motivated young girls with leadership potential. As a result, 20 fellows were selected for the program.
The selected fellows participated in a two-day orientation focused on leadership, capacity building, program goals, and their role as young change-makers.
2. Capacity Building Trainings
Participants received intensive training on key leadership and development themes, including:
•	Leadership and communication skills
•	Self-awareness and personal development
•	Gender and development
•	Education system analysis
•	Technology and digital safety
•	Research, advocacy, and lobbying
•	Idea development and action planning
These trainings helped fellows build confidence, understand community challenges, and develop practical solutions.
3. Practical Learning and Community Engagement
The program moved beyond classroom learning and encouraged fellows to apply their skills in real life. Fellows designed and implemented community-based projects and led awareness sessions on education, girls’ empowerment, and local development issues.
They engaged with students, parents, teachers, and community members to promote girls’ education and leadership.
4. Stakeholder Engagement
MRC strengthened collaboration with parents, educators, community leaders, education departments, and institutions. Quarterly stakeholder meetings were organized to discuss challenges in the education system and explore collective solutions.
This engagement helped build stronger community support for girls’ education and leadership.
Key Activities
Major activities of the program included:
•	Opening and graduation ceremonies
•	Leadership and capacity-building workshops
•	Community engagement sessions led by fellows
•	District-level STEM Science Festival for girls
•	Trainings for school heads, education officers, and activists
•	Quarterly stakeholder meetings
•	Awareness sessions on girls’ education and empowerment
•	Youth-led community projects
Impact
The Girls Leadership Program created meaningful change in the lives of young girls and their communities. Through training, mentoring, and practical exposure, 20 young girls were transformed into confident leaders and change-makers.
The program improved leadership, communication, public speaking, teamwork, and critical thinking skills among fellows. It also increased community awareness about girls’ education, empowerment, and the importance of supporting young women’s leadership.
The program strengthened collaboration between communities, schools, education stakeholders, and youth leaders, creating a stronger support system for girls in Khuzdar.
Outcomes
By the end of the program:
•	Fellows developed and implemented practical community projects.
•	Girls improved their confidence, public speaking, and decision-making abilities.
•	Parents and communities became more supportive of girls’ leadership.
•	Youth-led initiatives were developed to address local challenges.
•	A strong network of young female leaders was created in Khuzdar.
•	Fellows became more active in education advocacy, community engagement, and social change.
The Girls Leadership Program reflects MRC’s long-term commitment to empowering girls in Balochistan through leadership, learning, advocacy, and action. It proved that when girls are given the right skills, trust, and opportunities, they become powerful voices for change in their communities.

Her Power – Girls’ Leadership Program (2025)
Project Name.  Her Power
Partner: Women Fund Asia
Location:  Khuzdar ,Balochistan , Pakistan 
Duration: 1 years( Nov 2025 – oct 2025 

Supported by Women Fund Asia
Her Power is one of MRC’s flagship initiatives designed to identify, support, and nurture emerging young women leaders in Balochistan. The project focused on girls who have ideas, passion, and potential, but limited access to platforms, skills, and opportunities.
Through this program, MRC provided young women with leadership training, confidence-building sessions, entrepreneurship skills, communication support, and practical business development guidance. The project encouraged girls to move beyond learning and start taking action by turning their ideas into real businesses, start-ups, and community initiatives.
Out of 71 applicants, 20 young women were selected through interviews and assessment. By the end of the program, these participants became more confident, skilled, and ready to lead their own journey as founders, entrepreneurs, and community leaders.
Objectives of the Project
The main objectives of Her Power were to:
•	Build leadership and confidence among young women.
•	Support girls to identify their skills, ideas, and future goals.
•	Provide entrepreneurship and business development training.
•	Help young women turn their ideas into practical start-ups and initiatives.
•	Promote girls’ economic participation and decision-making.
•	Create a supportive network of young women leaders in Balochistan.
•	Inspire families and communities to trust and support girls’ leadership.
Girls-Led Start-ups and Initiatives
The Her Power leaders launched inspiring businesses and platforms, including:
•	Yad-e – by Sughra – Sughra Baloch
•	Crazy for Momos – Kalsoom

•	Stickers Studio – Mahrosh
•	Skill & Tech Fashion – Samreen
•	Mali Pop – Maliha
•	Youth for Youth – Avi Khatoon
•	Girls Corner Science Institute – Sehrish
•	Dream Jewelry Design – Taqdees

Results and Impact
At the beginning of the project, many participants had strong ideas but limited confidence, direction, and support. Through Her Power, they gained practical skills and the courage to take their first steps as leaders and entrepreneurs.
As a result of the project, participants are now:
•	20 Running small businesses and start-ups established .

School of Scholars, Khuzdar, Balochistan
Transforming Girls’ Education Since 2015
Founded by Mechanism for Rational Change (MRC) in 2015, the School of Scholars is a girls’ high school in Khuzdar, Balochistan. It was created to demonstrate how education can go beyond textbooks and prepare girls to become confident leaders, innovators, entrepreneurs, and active citizens.
Currently, 231 girls are enrolled in the school, while hundreds of girls have graduated and moved forward in their education, careers, leadership journeys, and community roles.
Known as a “Place of Daughters,” the school provides a safe, inspiring, and inclusive environment where girls are trusted to dream big, explore their talents, and shape their own futures. Through practical learning, leadership opportunities, and community engagement, students learn not only to succeed in school but also to contribute positively to society.
Our Goal
To build a model of quality, innovative, and girl-centred education that enables girls in Balochistan to access learning, develop life skills, pursue higher education, and become leaders in their communities.
Objectives
•	Strengthen girls’ access to safe, quality, and inclusive education.
•	Build confidence, communication, leadership, and decision-making skills.
•	Promote STEM learning, creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving.
•	Develop entrepreneurship, financial awareness, and enterprise skills.
•	Connect classroom learning with real-life community challenges.
•	Encourage girls’ participation in sports, climate action, technology, heritage, and civic life.
Innovative Initiatives
STEM and Innovation
Girls participate in hands-on science, technology, engineering, and mathematics activities through Science Clubs, Tech Lovers, Climate Action projects, Playing with Digits, experiments, and innovation challenges.
Leadership and Life Skills
Students develop public speaking, teamwork, self-awareness, communication, and decision-making skills from an early age. They learn to express their ideas, take responsibility, and lead with confidence.
Entrepreneurship and Enterprise
Through enterprise workshops, student business activities, and festivals, girls turn creative ideas into products and social initiatives. They learn budgeting, marketing, financial responsibility, and innovation.
Project-Based Learning
Learning is active and connected to real life. Students explore local issues, develop ideas, conduct projects, and present solutions related to education, climate, technology, health, and community development.
Student-Led Clubs and Platforms
Girls explore their interests and lead activities through:
•	Climate and environmental action
•	Science and technology clubs
•	Sports activities
•	Creative industries and handmade skills
•	Heritage learning about Khuzdar and Balochistan
•	Community service and civic engagement
•	Entrepreneurship and youth leadership platforms
Results and Impact
The School of Scholars has created visible change in the confidence, ambition, and participation of girls in Khuzdar. With 231 girls currently enrolled and hundreds of graduates, the school continues to build a growing network of educated, skilled, and confident young women.
Students are becoming peer mentors, community volunteers, youth advocates, innovators, and emerging entrepreneurs. Parents and communities increasingly see girls as capable decision-makers and future professionals.
The school has gained recognition as a successful rural model for reimagining education at local, national, and international platforms. For its outstanding contribution to girls’ education and empowerment, the School of Scholars received the Women Can Do Award from the U.S. Consulate Karachi.
Why Donor Support Matters
Investing in the School of Scholars means investing in girls who can transform families, communities, and the future of Balochistan. Support will help expand access to innovative learning spaces, STEM resources, leadership programmes, sports, mental-health support, teacher development, and opportunities for girls from underserved communities.
At the School of Scholars, every girl is encouraged to believe: “My ideas matter, my voice matters, and my future is possible.”

Future CEOs Project
Girls’ Entrepreneurship and Leadership Initiative
The Future CEOs Project was designed and implemented by Kidvation Global, Oklahoma, USA, in partnership with local stakeholders in Khuzdar, Balochistan. The initiative was created to nurture entrepreneurial thinking, leadership skills, creativity, and business confidence among young girls from Grade 5 to Grade 9.
Through a structured four-month learning journey, 20 selected students from Khuzdar completed intensive training under the mentorship of Mr. Brent Wheel Barger and the Kidvation Global team.
The project proved that children can become innovators, entrepreneurs, and leaders when they are given trust, guidance, and a platform to turn their ideas into action.
Our Goal
To empower young girls in Khuzdar with leadership, entrepreneurship, innovation, and financial literacy skills so they can confidently develop ideas, create solutions, and become future business and community leaders.
Objectives
•	Build self-awareness, confidence, and leadership skills among young girls.
•	Introduce students to entrepreneurship, business planning, and market understanding.
•	Strengthen creativity, innovation, teamwork, and problem-solving abilities.
•	Develop communication, presentation, and decision-making skills.
•	Promote financial literacy and responsible business thinking.
•	Provide a safe platform where girls can transform ideas into real products and enterprises.
Program Structure and Learning Journey
Participants attended 37 structured sessions focused on personal growth and enterprise development. The programme combined learning, mentorship, practical exercises, teamwork, and real-world application.
Key learning areas included:
•	Self-awareness and confidence building
•	Teamwork and collaboration
•	Leadership development
•	Idea creation and innovation
•	Business planning and product development
•	Communication and presentation skills
•	Financial basics and market understanding
From Ideas to Enterprises
At the end of the programme, students transformed their creative ideas into small enterprises and presented their products at a public Enterprise Festival.
Their businesses and products included:
•	Books and educational materials
•	STEM-based products
•	Stickers and creative designs
•	Bubbles and handmade crafts
•	Traditional foods and chocolates
•	Doll accessories
•	Resin artwork
Through this process, girls learned how to develop a product, understand customers, manage basic finances, present their ideas, and work as a team.
Results and Impact
The Future CEOs Project built much more than business skills. It strengthened girls’ confidence, leadership, creativity, communication, financial awareness, and ability to solve problems.
The 20 trained Future CEOs became role models for other students and inspired hundreds of young girls in Khuzdar. Many participants continued leading their small enterprises and applying their learning in their homes, schools, and communities.
The project demonstrated that rural girls have strong potential to become entrepreneurs and changemakers when they receive the right opportunities, mentorship, and practical learning spaces.
Why It Matters
The Future CEOs Project is a powerful example of global collaboration and local action. It showed that investment in girls’ ideas can create long-term change for families and communities.
By giving girls a platform to create, lead, earn, and innovate, the project helped them see themselves not only as students, but as future CEOs, business leaders, and problem-solvers for Balochistan.



STEM Pioneers Project – Kalat District (2023)
Project Brief
Supported by the U.S. Consulate and the Pakistan-U.S. Alumni Network (PUAN), the STEM Pioneers Project was a six-month initiative implemented by Mechanism for Rational Change (MRC) in Kalat District. The project promoted practical, inclusive, and future-focused STEM education in five girls’ high schools by strengthening teachers’ skills, introducing digital learning opportunities, and creating STEM learning spaces for students.
Goal
To inspire girls in Kalat District to explore science, technology, engineering, and mathematics as pathways to education, leadership, innovation, and future careers.
Objectives
•	Strengthen STEM knowledge and practical teaching skills among teachers.
•	Increase girls’ interest, confidence, and participation in STEM subjects.
•	Develop student STEM Ambassadors with leadership, communication, and digital skills.
•	Establish STEM and computer labs in participating schools.
•	Build collaboration among schools through a sustainable STEM Education Network.
Key Results
•	Engaged 5 girls’ high schools, 80 students, and 20 teachers.
•	Trained STEM Ambassadors in STEM concepts, leadership, event planning, and IT skills.
•	Conducted STEM awareness sessions, intensive trainings, IT courses, and an exposure visit to Magnifi Science Center.
•	Established STEM and computer labs, giving many girls access to hands-on learning tools and computers for the first time.
•	Improved teachers’ use of interactive and inquiry-based teaching methods.
•	Increased students’ confidence, curiosity, peer learning, and interest in STEM-related careers.
•	Created a STEM Education Network to continue collaboration and STEM activities beyond the project period.
